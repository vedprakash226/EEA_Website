DUVVURI SUBBARAO: A Stalwart Alumnus of IIT Kanpur

  1. Home
  2. Blogs

In the realm of distinguished personalities who have left an indelible mark on India's economic landscape, Duvvuri Subbarao stands as a beacon of excellence. Born on 11th August 1949 in Eluru, Andhra Pradesh, Subbarao is not only a seasoned economist and retired IAS officer but also the 22nd Governor of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). As we delve into the narrative of his life, we discover a journey marked by academic brilliance, impactful administrative roles, and a commitment to public service.

Subbarao's roots trace back to the scenic town of Eluru in West Godavari district, where he spent his formative years. His academic journey commenced at Sainik School, Korukonda, Andhra Pradesh, and took a formidable turn when he pursued BSc Hons in Physics at the Institute of Technology Kharagpur, graduating at the top of his class. Subsequently, he earned an MSc at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, a prestigious institution known for nurturing some of the brightest minds in the country.

His ascendancy to the zenith of the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) in 1972 marked the beginning of a remarkable career. Notably, during 1977–78, Subbarao embarked on a journey of academic enrichment at the Ohio State University, United States, pursuing a master's degree in economics. Later, as a Humphrey Fellow at MIT, he delved into the intricacies of public finance. In 1988, he earned his Ph.D. in economics from Andhra University, focusing on fiscal reforms at the sub-national level.

Subbarao's early career involved impactful field postings in Andhra Pradesh, where his meticulous implementation of land transfer regulations in Khammam District earned him a formidable reputation. His tenure as the Managing Director of Andhra Pradesh State Finance Corporation (1986–88) was marked by encouragement for first-generation entrepreneurs to invest in industry and service sectors.

The trajectory of Subbarao's career took an international turn when he worked as a lead economist at the World Bank (1999-2004). His responsibilities included encouraging public expenditure reforms in developing countries, and he played a pivotal role in managing the World Bank's flagship study on decentralization in East Asia.

Returning to India, Subbarao's stint as the Finance Secretary to the Government of Andhra Pradesh (1993–98) showcased his leadership in pathbreaking fiscal reforms. His instrumental role in publishing a white paper on state finances in 1996 garnered popular support for economically challenging reforms.

As a distinguished alumnus of IIT Kanpur, Subbarao's connection with his alma mater remains strong. He is a proud recipient of the Distinguished Alumnus Award in 2009, a testament to his exemplary contributions to the fields of economics and public administration.

Beyond the realms of academia and administration, Subbarao's personal life adds a touch of warmth to his illustrious journey. Married to Urmila Subbarao, the couple shares the joy of parenthood with two sons, Mallik and Raghav, both of whom, like their father, are IIT graduates. This familial connection to IIT Kanpur highlights the institution's legacy of producing not only exceptional professionals but also fostering a tradition of excellence through generations.

In conclusion, Duvvuri Subbarao's narrative is a testament to the transformative power of education and the impact one individual can have on the nation. As IIT Kanpur takes pride in its vast and prosperous alumni network, Subbarao emerges as a shining example of the institution's commitment to shaping leaders who leave an indelible imprint on the world.
